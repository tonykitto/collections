An Introduction to the Architecture of Towneley Hall by Susan Bourne

"An Architectural History of Towneley Hall" by W. John and Kit Smith, published by Heritage Trust for the North West in 2004, provides a detailed description of the house. The much shorter "An Introduction to the Architectural History of Towneley Hall, Burnley" by Susan Bourne, published in 1979, provides a good overview. A pdf version of Susan Bourne's book can be downloaded here.

Here are links to a short history of some of the individual rooms of the house

  • The Chapel, built in the 1530s, was rebuilt in its present position in the north wing around 1712
  • The Long Gallery on the upper floor of the mediaeval south wing took its present form with bedrooms overlooking the gardens around 1700.
  • The Great Hall was created around 1620 but was rebuilt in 1725 with its interior decoration being completed in 1731.
  • One of the rooms that no longer exists is the the library room.
